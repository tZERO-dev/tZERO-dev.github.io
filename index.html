Security Token Standard

The standard is comprised of the following three, interconnected components:

  1. Registry
  2. Compliance
  3. Token

These components provide a shared, reusable investor data cache, along with a collection of composable compliance rules, each of which enhance the tradeability of assets at each token.

Global Registry

The registry is a single instance smart-contract, useable by all assets within the STS ecosystem. Its main purpose is to house a heirarchy of market participant data, such as accreditation and domicile.

The heirarchy allows for the enforcement of who may add, remove, and update participant data. For example, a brokerage may manage their investors, but are restricted from applying any modifications outside of their hierarchy .

Compliance

A regulatory enforcement engine, managing which rules to compose for a particular asset. Each rule must be satisfied for a transfer to succeed. The collection of rules are dynamic, and may be modified depending on the requirements and needs of an asset.

Token

Token assets are decorated implementations of existing standards, such as ERC-20 or FA1.2. This allows for broad interoperability while providing the enhancements of whitelist checking and regulatory rule enforcement provided by the components of the standard.
